Frequently Asked Questions

We are against electric cars due to their negative impact on the environment during production, dependency on finite resources for their batteries, and lack of adequate infrastructure for charging.

We propose promoting the use of public transportation, carpooling, walking, biking, and opting for vehicles powered by renewable fuels such as hydrogen or biofuels.

The manufacturing of electric cars involves the extraction of minerals such as lithium and cobalt, whose exploitation can lead to deforestation, soil degradation, and water pollution.

To reduce the use of electric cars, we advocate for efficient public transportation policies, investment in infrastructure for sustainable transportation modes, and raising awareness about the environmental impacts of electric cars.

Fuel-powered cars offer greater range, faster refueling times, and a more developed refueling infrastructure compared to electric cars.
